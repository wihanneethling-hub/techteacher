# Blog Content Audit: Tags and Categories

## Executive Summary

The Tech Teacher archive currently contains **76 blog posts**. The content is heavily weighted toward **Technology** and **Leadership**, with **AI** and **Parents** still relatively small sections.

The category structure itself is sensible: AI, Technology, Leadership and Parents are clear top-level buckets. The main issue is not the category model, but the imported Blogger metadata. Many older posts have either no tags or broad legacy tags such as `EdTech`, `school` and `Instagram` that do not help readers browse or search meaningfully.

The biggest practical findings:

- **Technology dominates the archive** with 45 posts.
- **Leadership is substantial** with 23 posts, but some tool-specific posts are currently in Leadership.
- **AI has only 6 posts**, and two of them are arguably broader EdTech or consumer tech posts rather than AI-first posts.
- **Parents has only 2 posts**, even though several older posts mention children, families, screen time or primary-school learners.
- **37 posts have no tags**, which limits search, filtering and SEO context.
- Legacy tags are inconsistent in casing and meaning, especially `EdTech`, `tech`, `Tech Tips`, `school`, `teachers`, `training`, `ai`, `ChatGPT`, `google` and `GSuite`.

Editorially, the site would benefit from a cleaner tag vocabulary that sits underneath the four public categories. Tags should describe useful reader themes, not migration artefacts.

## Suggested Tag and Category Cleanup

### Recommended category model

Keep the four public categories:

- **AI**: AI in education, ChatGPT, generative AI, AI literacy, AI policy, AI tools and classroom impact.
- **Technology**: Practical tools, apps, devices, workflows, tutorials, Google/Apple/Microsoft tools, streaming and classroom tech tips.
- **Leadership**: Strategy, professional learning, change management, staff development, school leadership, digital transformation and implementation planning.
- **Parents**: Family technology use, screen time, social media, online safety, digital wellbeing, children and devices, AI at home.

### Archive cleanup opportunities

Several imported Instagram-style posts are short and have similar metadata. They may be worth:

- Keeping as historical archive items but tagging consistently.
- Grouping under `remote learning` or `Instagram archive`.
- Excluding very thin posts from future featured lists if they are not useful to current readers.
- Editing descriptions so search results look more polished.
