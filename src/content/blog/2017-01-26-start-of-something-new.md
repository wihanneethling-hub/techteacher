Hello World!

Creating and running a blog is something that many people have attempted to do, but quite often it starts off with a bang and then fades away reasonably quickly. With this blog, I am going to attempt to buck the trend and update regularly - using it as a place to document my experiences as a Technology Integration Specialist and hopefully offer some advice on what works and what doesn't!

I will aim to provide regular tips that I have found to make my life easier, as well as those of the teachers in my school. My background as a classroom teacher means that I know what works practically in the classroom, and that I will not recommend or endorse something just because it is the latest fad in a fast growing industry.

[
](https://www.google.com/edu/products/productivity-tools/) [G Suite for Education](https://www.google.com/edu/products/productivity-tools/) is reportedly being used by over [70 Million people](https://www.google.co.za/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=&esrc=s&source=web&cd=&cad=rja&uact=8&ved=0ahUKEwilkPeI6t_RAhUFLcAKHQ_tAy8QFggaMAA&url=https%3A%2F%2F9to5google.com%2F2017%2F01%2F24%2Fg-suite-for-education-70-million%2F&usg=AFQjCNGIMz_doqxJCOd4CgI2AP8y_2sFng&sig2=uKXYTnZCxdZHDUKTpsTkMQ), and [Microsoft Classroom](https://www.google.co.za/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=&esrc=s&source=web&cd=&cad=rja&uact=8&ved=0ahUKEwiY54PI6t_RAhUsKsAKHXQ9DWsQFggaMAA&url=https%3A%2F%2Fclassroom.microsoft.com%2F&usg=AFQjCNEegVauRyy0i-NhJMMF0_R1Ness2Q&sig2=43XggGDvIm7Dw34l3dHGWQ) is also picking up steam thanks to their foothold and experience in enterprise. We are currently running a trial of both of these systems, with a small pilot group of teachers using either one or both with their classes. We are hoping to establish which one of these cloud-based solutions will provide us with the best way to take learning outside the classroom.

I will be updating our progress regularly, so stay tuned!

'till next time.
